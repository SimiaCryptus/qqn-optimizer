Conclusions and Recommendations

Key Findings

  • The L-BFGS optimizer demonstrated the best overall performance across the test suite.
  • QQN variants showed improved robustness compared to standard L-BFGS on ill-conditioned problems.
  • The parametric interpolation approach successfully balances gradient descent and quasi-Newton information.
  • No single optimizer dominated across all problem types, highlighting the importance of adaptive methods.

Recommendations for Practitioners

  • Use QQN-Default for general-purpose optimization with unknown problem characteristics.
  • Consider QQN-Conservative for highly ill-conditioned or noisy problems.
  • L-BFGS remains competitive for well-conditioned convex problems.
  • Always run multiple random seeds and report statistical significance.
